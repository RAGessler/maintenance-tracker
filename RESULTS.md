## Design observations

- iOS Shortcuts provides CarPlay connect and disconnect triggers.
- On the physical test iPhone, Shortcuts provides selected-device Bluetooth connect and disconnect
  triggers for `GTA Car Kit`.
- The app does not start trips from `AVAudioSession` notifications. An explicit App Intent is
  required, preventing unrelated AirPods or speaker route changes from creating a trip.
- Native code is the only writer to the SQLite evidence database. JavaScript reads diagnostics via
  the module, avoiding cross-runtime transaction rollback races.
- A selected car route is captured when available. Its disappearance creates an end candidate and a
  three-minute reconnect deadline when an explicit disconnect intent is delayed or missed; an
  in-process timer, restoration, or any location callback after expiration finalizes the trip.
- Candidate and route-capture deadlines are persisted when movement or the configured car route
  cannot be confirmed. Deadline checks run on restoration, callbacks, and an in-process timer, but
  iOS suspension may defer expiration until the app's next execution opportunity.

## Car identity observations

Physical route evidence collected on 2026-07-23 showed that the CarPlay port name is the generic
`CarPlay` for both tested vehicles. The complete `AVAudioSession` route UID changed across
reconnects, so it is not a stable vehicle identifier as-is.

| Vehicle | Connection | Observed UID prefix | Reconnect behavior | Assessment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Car A | CarPlay | `00:00:00:00:00:00-Audio-AudioMain` | Final numeric segment changed between connections | Weak heuristic; all-zero prefix may collide |
| Car B | CarPlay | `94:BA:06:72:72:56-Audio-AudioMain` | Final numeric segment changed between connections | Promising heuristic; needs reboot/repeat validation |
| Car C | Bluetooth only | `00:18:E4:DC:DA:D7-tacl` | Not part of CarPlay testing | Stronger Bluetooth route fingerprint; port name `GTA Car Kit` |

The development build now labels these three observed fingerprints as Car A, Car B, and Car C.
CarPlay matching removes only the changing final numeric `AudioMain` segment. These are hardcoded
experimental heuristics, not durable vehicle identities; a route that does not match is shown as
unknown, and Car A's all-zero prefix is especially collision-prone.

Trip summaries calculate estimated distance from accepted GPS fixes. Consecutive segments shorter
than the reported horizontal accuracy (with a 5 m minimum) are ignored. This reduces stationary
jitter but requires an odometer comparison before the result can be treated as mileage.

For Cars A and B, the portion following `AudioMain-` changed on every observed reconnect. The
prefix before that suffix remained stable for each vehicle in this single session and distinguished
the two vehicles. This is an undocumented, experimental fingerprint only: the app must not claim
that it identifies a vehicle until it remains stable after iPhone and head-unit reboots and across
multiple drives.

`GTA Car Kit` is Car C's Bluetooth-only audio device, not either CarPlay vehicle. It should be
configured with selected-device Bluetooth connect and disconnect Shortcuts automations, not used to
identify Cars A or B.
- Expo documents that background location stops after user termination. Force-quit behavior must be
  treated as a limitation unless physical testing proves a narrower supported case.

## Physical results

### Validated CarPlay behavior

Physical testing on 2026-07-23 validates the core CarPlay spike on a signed development build:

- Car A started while the app was backgrounded and the phone was fully locked, without foreground
  interaction. It progressed from candidate to active tracking after movement.
- Car A was recognized across multiple connections despite changing `AudioMain` numeric suffixes.
- Car A reconnect restored an active trip from reconnect grace. Route loss later completed the trip
  through the configured CarPlay disconnect flow.
- Car B was recognized as a distinct vehicle using its normalized CarPlay UID prefix. Its recorded
  run remained in candidate state because the highest sample speed was `2.99 m/s`, below the `3 m/s`
  activation threshold; this does not invalidate its identification result.
- GPS distance was compared against the Car A odometer: the phone reported `0.94 mi` while the car
  displayed `0.9 mi`. Since the car display is only precise to `0.1 mi`, this is a promising
  preliminary accuracy result, not a calibrated mileage guarantee.

The remaining distinct trigger path to validate is Car C's Bluetooth-only stereo. The physical
test iPhone confirms that selected-device Bluetooth connect and disconnect automations are
available for `GTA Car Kit`, so Car C should exercise the same start/end App Intent flow as CarPlay
while also confirming its Bluetooth route fingerprint and locked-screen behavior.
